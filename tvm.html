THIRUVANANTHAPURAM

Thiruvananthapuram is located on the west coast of India near the extreme south of the mainland. Referred to by Mahatma Gandhi as the "Evergreen city of India," the city is characterized by its undulating terrain of low coastal hills. The city is classified as a Tier-II city by the government of India. Trivandrum is the capital city of the Indian state of Kerala. It is located on the southwestern coast of India, along the Arabian Sea. Thiruvananthapuram is the largest city in Kerala and serves as the administrative and political center of the state. Thiruvananthapuram is known for its beautiful coastal location, with many beaches, including Kovalam and Varkala, being popular tourist destinations. The city has a tropical climate. The city's economy is diverse, with contributions from government services, education, information technology, tourism, and healthcare sectors. Malayalam is the primary language spoken in Thiruvananthapuram, but English is widely understood and used for official and educational purposes.
